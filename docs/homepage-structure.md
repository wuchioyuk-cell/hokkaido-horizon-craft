# Homepage Structure

## Role Of This Document

This document defines the purpose and expected role of every homepage section.

Before modifying homepage code, always read:

- `AGENTS.md`
- `docs/design-bible.md`
- `docs/design-system.md`
- `docs/homepage-structure.md`

Only modify the section explicitly requested. Do not improve unrelated sections.

## Homepage Goal

The homepage should introduce J-ONE Horizon / Hokkaido Horizon as a local Hokkaido travel studio and travel concierge.

The visitor should quickly understand:

- This is not luxury travel.
- This is not a discount travel agency.
- This is not a generic package-tour company.
- This is a local Hokkaido planning partner.
- Accommodation, transportation, routes, seasonal journeys, and ski experiences can be planned together.

The homepage should make one idea clear:

`Hokkaido, Don't Rush.`

## Primary Navigation

Use this navigation structure:

- Stay
- Journeys
- Experiences
- Snow
- About
- Contact

Snow should remain visible as a primary business pillar.

## 1. Hero

### Purpose

Create the first emotional impression: quiet, local, spacious, human, and photography-led.

### Message

The Hero should carry the brand philosophy:

`Hokkaido, Don't Rush.`

### What It Should Communicate

- This is a local Hokkaido travel studio.
- The journey begins with where guests stay and how they move.
- Hokkaido changes with season, weather, snow, and distance.
- The brand helps visitors slow down and plan better.

### Content Direction

Preferred:

- Short label such as `Local Hokkaido Travel Studio`
- Main title: `Hokkaido, Don't Rush.`
- Short supporting copy about stays, movement, season, and pace
- One or two calm actions at most

Avoid:

- Luxury hotel language
- Discount language
- Statistics
- Icons
- Dense product grids
- Urgency copy
- Generic AI landing page structure

### Visual Direction

Preferred:

- Full-screen or near full-screen photography
- Strong Hokkaido image
- Readable overlay
- Large editorial typography
- Minimal interface
- Lots of whitespace
- Subtle motion only

## 2. Travel Philosophy

### Purpose

Explain why Hokkaido should not be rushed.

### What It Should Communicate

A better Hokkaido trip is shaped by distance, road time, season, weather, daylight, snow, luggage, and energy. The brand helps travelers make choices that feel calmer and more realistic.

### Tone

Editorial, reflective, and human. Not sales-heavy.

## 3. Connected Planning

### Purpose

Explain the practical value of planning the whole trip as one connected system.

### What It Should Communicate

J-ONE Horizon / Hokkaido Horizon helps connect:

- Accommodation
- Transportation
- Airport transfers
- Private routes
- Seasonal experiences
- Snow and ski needs

This section should show that the brand is useful, not just beautiful.

## 4. Stay

### Purpose

Introduce accommodation as part of the journey.

### What It Should Communicate

Where travelers stay affects route timing, airport transfers, snow access, luggage movement, and daily comfort.

### Avoid

Do not make Stay feel like luxury hotel advertising. Keep it practical, local, and warm.

## 5. Journeys

### Purpose

Introduce private routes and slower Hokkaido journeys.

### What It Should Communicate

Journeys should feel shaped around pace, scenery, weather, road time, and personal travel style.

### Avoid

Do not make this section feel like a packed itinerary catalog.

## 6. Experiences

### Purpose

Introduce seasonal Hokkaido experiences beyond simple sightseeing.

### What It Should Communicate

Experiences change by season and should be chosen with timing, route, and travel style in mind.

### Tone

Specific, calm, and editorial. Avoid generic "top attractions" language.

## 7. Snow

### Purpose

Keep Snow visible as a major business pillar.

### What It Should Communicate

Snow is central to the business, not a small seasonal add-on.

Snow may include:

- Ski experiences
- Snow travel
- Winter route planning
- Ski resort access
- Lessons or local support
- Winter transfers
- Luggage and timing support
- Weather-aware planning

### Visual Direction

Use snow photography and clear practical language. Do not reduce Snow to a decorative winter mood.

## 8. About

### Purpose

Build trust through local presence and human planning.

### What It Should Communicate

The brand understands Hokkaido's routes, seasons, accommodation, transport, snow conditions, and guest needs.

### Avoid

Avoid grand luxury claims, founder mythology, or overly formal company language.

## 9. Contact

### Purpose

Invite visitors to start planning in a calm, low-pressure way.

### What It Should Ask For

Useful inquiry prompts can include:

- Travel dates
- Number of travelers
- Preferred pace
- Accommodation needs
- Transportation needs
- Places of interest
- Snow or ski needs
- Luggage situation
- Children or older travelers

### Tone

Helpful and human. Not aggressive.

## Footer

### Purpose

Close the page with clarity.

### Content Direction

The footer should support:

- Brand name
- Navigation
- Contact path
- Basic service categories

It should not become a sales banner.

## Homepage Editing Rules

- Preserve the homepage's calm editorial rhythm.
- Do not add unrelated sections without approval.
- Do not turn the homepage into a service catalog.
- Do not make Snow secondary.
- Do not add luxury, discount, SaaS, or generic travel-agency patterns.
- If a requested change conflicts with the design bible, stop and explain why.
